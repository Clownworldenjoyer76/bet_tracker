Kelly: X% means the formula recommends wagering X% of your total bankroll on that specific bet. It is a mathematical calculation designed to balance maximizing growth while minimizing the risk of going broke.

How the Calculation Works
The Kelly Criterion uses the relationship between your perceived Edge (the advantage you have over the bookmaker) and the Odds offered.

The Edge: You have a 35.19% edge on that Juventus/Pisa game.

The Recommendation: Because your calculated edge is high, the formula suggests a relatively aggressive stake of 8.25%.

The Goal: If you bet less than 8.25%, your bankroll grows slower than it could. If you bet more than 8.25%, you are over-leveraged and a string of losses could wipe you out.

Key Considerations
In sports betting, 8.25% is considered a very high single-game stake. Most professional bettors use "Fractional Kelly" (e.g., 1/4 Kelly) to protect against:

Model Error: If your "expected goals" or "edge" calculations are slightly off, the 8.25% stake becomes dangerously high.

Variance: Even with a massive edge, soccer has high variance. Losing an 8.25% bet is a significant hit to your total funds.

Note: The "Kelly Stake %" column in your CSV is essentially telling you the mathematical confidence in the bet relative to the price.
